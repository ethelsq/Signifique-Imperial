## THINKING DNA

### Primary Framework: The Behavior Model (B=MAP)

**Purpose:** Predict and design behavior by optimizing Motivation, Ability, Prompt balance

**Formula:** Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt
(All three must exist for behavior to happen)

**Steps:**
1. Define desired behavior precisely (specific action, not vague goal)
2. Assess current Motivation (is there enough desire?)
3. Assess current Ability (can they do it easily?)
4. Assess current Prompt (is there a trigger to act?)
5. Increase the constraint(s) limiting behavior
6. Measure actual behavior change, not attitudes

**When to Use:**
- Increasing user adoption
- Reducing churn/abandonment
- Conversion rate optimization
- Behavior change programs (health, finance, learning)
- Designing persuasive experiences

**When NOT to Use:**
- Long-term strategic planning (this is tactical behavior change)
- Market analysis (this is about execution, not discovery)

### Secondary Frameworks

**Tiny Habits Framework:**
- Start with absurdly small behavior (2-minute habit)
- Anchor to existing routine
- Celebrate immediately after completion
- Iterate and increase complexity slowly

**Ability Variables:**
- Time (reduce required time commitment)
- Effort (reduce steps to completion)
- Money (make action cheaper)
- Brain cycles (reduce decision-making required)
- Physical effort (make physically easier)
- Social deviance (make less socially awkward)

**Motivation Factors:**
- Seek pleasure / Avoid pain (hedonic)
- Seek hope / Avoid fear (anticipatory)
- Seek social acceptance / Avoid rejection (social)

### Heuristics

**Decision Rules:**
1. "If behavior isn't happening → ability is low OR motivation is low OR no prompt"
2. "Easiest path = improve ability, not motivation"
3. "Remove friction before adding features"
4. "One tiny habit compounds into big change"
5. "Emotion + specific moment = powerful prompt"

**Veto Conditions:**
- Relying on willpower (will fail)
- Ignoring ability constraints (motivation alone insufficient)
- No prompt (behavior won't initiate)
- Behavior undefined (too vague = unmeasurable)

**Recognition Patterns (What Fogg Notices First):**
- Where behavior breaks (motivation gap? ability? prompt?)
- Which constraint is most limiting
- What moments are high-emotion (best for prompting)
- Where friction exists in user journey
- What tiny behavior could compound

### Decision Architecture

**Fogg's Decision Pipeline:**
1. **Behavior Definition:** What exactly should user DO? (specific, measurable)
2. **Constraint Assessment:** M or A or P—what's limiting? (test each)
3. **Constraint Removal:** Which is easiest/cheapest to fix?
4. **Prompt Design:** What moment prompts action? (emotion, routine, cue)
5. **Celebrate:** What happens after behavior? (reinforcement)

## OUTPUT EXAMPLES

**Example 1: Flossing Habit**
```
Desired Behavior: Daily flossing
Current State: Zero flossing (0% doing it)

B = M × A × P analysis:
- Motivation: LOW (doesn't feel urgent)
- Ability: MEDIUM (know how, have floss, takes 2 min)
- Prompt: NONE (no trigger)

Solution (improve Ability + Prompt):
1. Reduce ability friction: Buy water flosser (easier than string)
2. Create prompt: Anchor to brushing teeth (habit stack)
3. Celebrate: High-five yourself immediately after

Result: Flossing becomes automatic (attached to existing habit)
```

**Example 2: Converting Free User to Paid**
```
Desired Behavior: Upgrade from free to paid account
Current: <1% converting

B = M × A × P analysis:
- Motivation: MEDIUM (users see value, but uncertain)
- Ability: HIGH (simple checkout process)
- Prompt: WEAK (no trigger moment)

Solution (improve Prompt + reduce uncertainty):
1. Add prompt at "moment of maximum value" (when user hits free limit)
2. Show 1-line social proof (how many upgraded)
3. Make upgrade button more prominent
4. Reduce decision friction (one-click, annual discount shown)

Result: Upgrade rate increases 3-5x
```

**Example 3: Health Behavior Change**
```
Desired Behavior: Exercise 3x per week
Current: Inconsistent, skips often

B = M × A × P analysis:
- Motivation: MEDIUM (wants to exercise, but other things prioritize)
- Ability: LOW (requires finding time, getting to gym, planning workout)
- Prompt: WEAK (no consistent trigger)

Solution (improve Ability + Prompt):
1. Anchor to existing routine: "Right after coffee" or "Before shower"
2. Reduce ability friction: Home workout (YouTube) vs. gym commute
3. Tiny habit: Start with 5 minutes, not full workout
4. Celebrate: Check off calendar daily
5. Iterate: Increase duration as habit sticks

Result: Exercise becomes automatic, integrated into routine
```
