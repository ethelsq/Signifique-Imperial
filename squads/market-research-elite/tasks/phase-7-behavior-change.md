## B=MAP Framework

### MOTIVATION

**Three Types:**

1. **Seek Pleasure / Avoid Pain** (Hedonic)
   - Feel good vs. avoid discomfort
   - Immediate, emotional

2. **Seek Hope / Avoid Fear** (Anticipatory)
   - Future possibility vs. anxiety
   - Medium-term

3. **Seek Acceptance / Avoid Rejection** (Social)
   - Belonging vs. isolation
   - Social pressure/support

**Reality:** Most people have MEDIUM motivation
- Don't rely on motivation (it changes)
- Design for low motivation scenarios

### ABILITY

**Six Factors (Reduce ANY to make behavior easier):**

1. **Time** - Reduce time commitment
2. **Effort** - Reduce steps/complexity
3. **Money** - Make cheaper
4. **Brain Cycles** - Reduce thinking required
5. **Physical Effort** - Make physically easier
6. **Social Deviance** - Make less socially awkward

**Reality:** Easiest path = improve ability
- Even small ability improvements big impact
- Motivation is harder to change

### PROMPT

**When behavior is triggered:**

- **At moment of high motivation** (ideal)
- **Anchored to existing routine** (reliable)
- **High emotional state** (memorable)
- **Repeated exposure** (habitual)

**Reality:** Prompt must be RELIABLE
- Users forget without reminder
- Anchor to existing behavior = automatic

## Analysis Process

**Step 1: Define Behavior**
- Specific action (not vague goal)
- Example: NOT "Exercise more" BUT "Walk 15 min after breakfast"
- Measurable, observable

**Step 2: Assess M, A, P**
- Motivation: 1-10 scale (how much do they want it?)
- Ability: 1-10 scale (how easy is it?)
- Prompt: Exists? Reliable?

**Step 3: Identify Constraint**
- Which is LOWEST?
- That's your limiting factor
- Focus there first

**Step 4: Design Intervention**
- If Motivation low: Will motivation approach work? (Unlikely - address ability instead)
- If Ability low: Reduce friction (time, effort, steps, thinking)
- If Prompt weak: Create reliable trigger, anchor to routine

## Example: Exercise Behavior

**Desired Behavior:** Walk 15 min after breakfast

**B=MAP:**
- Motivation: 6/10 (Want to but busy)
- Ability: 7/10 (Know how, have time)
- Prompt: 2/10 (No reliable trigger)

**Limiting Constraint:** Prompt (no trigger)

**Friction Reduction:**
- Anchor to breakfast (existing routine)
- Prepare walking clothes night before
- Share goal with accountability partner
- Celebrate with coffee after walk

**Result:** Walking becomes automatic (attached to breakfast)

## When This Phase Fails

**If gate is NO:**

- Behavior still has too much friction
- Problem wasn't B=MAP (check earlier phases)
- Constraint misidentified
- Intervention too ambitious (not tiny enough)

**What to do:**
1. Return to B=MAP analysis
2. Identify real constraint
3. Design smaller intervention
4. Test with real users
5. Iterate
